
Bright Motors Car Sales Analysis 🚗📊
Project Overview

This project analyzes historical vehicle sales data for Bright Motors to uncover business insights that can improve sales performance, optimize inventory management, and support strategic decision-making.

As part of this case study, I worked as a Junior Data Analyst to clean, transform, analyze, and visualize car sales data using SQL, Excel, and Power BI.

The analysis focuses on:

Revenue performance by vehicle make and model
Regional sales performance
Customer purchasing preferences
Pricing trends based on mileage and vehicle age
Profitability opportunities for the dealership network
Business Problem

Bright Motors appointed a new Head of Sales with the goal of:

Expanding dealership operations
Increasing sales performance
Improving inventory optimization
Enhancing profitability

The objective of this project was to transform raw sales data into actionable insights that support data-driven business decisions.

Dataset Information
Dataset Name: Bright Car Sales

Data Cleaning & Transformation

The following transformations were performed:

Converted text-based prices into numeric format
Removed inconsistencies and handled missing values
Created calculated columns:
total_revenue
profit_margin
Categorized vehicles into:
High Margin
Medium Margin
Low Margin
Grouped sales data by month, quarter, and year
Key Business Insights
Executive Summary
Total Revenue: $7.61 Billion
Vehicles Sold: 558.8K
Average Selling Price: $13,611
Average Margin vs MMR: -15.1%
Top Revenue Generating Brands

The top-performing manufacturers were:

Ford
Chevrolet
Nissan

These three brands generated 35.8% of total revenue.

Best Performing Regions

The strongest sales regions included:

Florida (FL)
California (CA)
Pennsylvania (PA)
Texas (TX)
Customer Preferences

Key purchasing trends showed:

Sedans and SUVs generated the highest revenue
Automatic transmission vehicles dominated sales
Most vehicles fell into the medium performance tier
Pricing Trends

Analysis showed:

Vehicle prices decrease significantly as mileage increases
Newer model years command higher average selling prices
Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

Increase inventory and marketing focus on top-performing brands
Improve pricing discipline to reduce negative margins
Expand dealership investment in high-performing regions
Reposition high-mileage vehicles using warranties and trade-in offers
Align procurement strategy with SUV and Sedan demand
